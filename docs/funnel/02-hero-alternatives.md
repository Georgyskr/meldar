# Hero Section Alternatives

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Based on:** Research synthesis (20 agents, 13 life categories)
**Constraint:** Non-technical audience, no jargon, under 12 words, gradient accent phrase

---

## Option 1 (Recommended)

### Headline

> You know what annoys you. **_We make it disappear._**

Gradient accent: "We make it disappear."

### Subheadline variations

1. "Describe any daily frustration. Meldar builds a personal tool that handles it for you — in 30 minutes, with zero setup."
2. "That thing you waste 30 minutes on every day? Tell Meldar about it. It builds something that does it for you."
3. "Meal planning, expense tracking, inbox chaos — tell us what drains your time. We build your fix."

**Why this works:** The research shows the #1 pattern is "manual tracking fatigue" — people know exactly what annoys them but have no way to fix it without technical skill. "You know what annoys you" validates their frustration instantly. "We make it disappear" is the payoff — specific, visceral, and promises elimination, not improvement.

---

## Option 2

### Headline

> Tired of doing the same thing every day? **_Stop._**

Gradient accent: "Stop."

### Subheadline variations

1. "Tell Meldar what eats your time. It builds a personal app that takes over — no skills required."
2. "Copying data, checking websites, tracking receipts — describe what you're sick of. Meldar handles the rest."
3. "You shouldn't have to remember, check, or repeat. Describe the chore. Meldar builds your way out."

**Why this works:** Directly mirrors the "polling to push" and "manual tracking fatigue" patterns. People refreshing grade portals 10 times a day, manually posting to 4 social accounts, checking apartment sites every morning — they're exhausted by repetition. The single-word gradient accent "Stop." is arresting and gives them permission to quit the hamster wheel.

---

## Option 3

### Headline

> Tell us what wastes your time. **_We'll build your fix._**

Gradient accent: "We'll build your fix."

### Subheadline variations

1. "Meldar turns your daily frustrations into personal apps that work for you — no experience needed."
2. "Describe any repetitive task in plain words. In 30 minutes you'll have a tool that does it automatically."
3. "Grocery planning, invoice chasing, job applications — whatever drains you. Meldar creates your personal solution."

**Why this works:** Most direct cause-and-effect framing. Research showed competitors either require you to know what to build (Lovable, Bolt) or force you through complex setup (Zapier/Make). This headline says: just complain, and we handle the building. The word "fix" implies broken → solved, not "new tool to learn."

---

## Option 4

### Headline

> Your life has repetitive tasks. **_Now they have an end date._**

Gradient accent: "Now they have an end date."

### Subheadline variations

1. "Describe any chore that eats your week. Meldar builds a personal tool to handle it — in plain language, no experience needed."
2. "From meal planning to expense tracking to inbox overload — tell Meldar what's draining you. It builds the solution."
3. "You started tracking calories, expenses, job applications — then quit. Meldar keeps going where you left off."

**Why this works:** Research identified that people start tracking habits and quit within 2 weeks because the effort exceeds 30 seconds/day. This headline reframes automation as finality — not "a better tool" but "the end of the task entirely." The third subheadline directly references the tracking-and-quitting pattern from the research.

---

## Option 5

### Headline

> You quit tracking because it took too long. **_Meldar never quits._**

Gradient accent: "Meldar never quits."

### Subheadline variations

1. "Calories, expenses, job applications — you started and stopped. Tell Meldar what to track. It keeps going forever."
2. "Describe any task you gave up on. Meldar builds a personal tool that handles it automatically, every single day."
3. "The diet tracker you abandoned. The budget spreadsheet that died. Meldar picks up where you left off — and never stops."

**Why this works:** This is the most emotionally targeted option. The research found "manual tracking fatigue" across 6 of 13 life categories — fitness, finance, hobbies, jobs, freelance, students. Everyone has started and quit something. This headline meets them at that moment of guilt/frustration and offers redemption. High risk, high reward — it could feel accusatory to some, but deeply seen by others.
