## Section 2: Problem

**Heading:**
> Be honest. This is your week.

**Pain point 1:**
- Title: **You hit a wall every time**
- Body: You heard AI can save you hours. You tried. Then it asked you to install three things you'd never heard of and enter codes into a black screen. You closed the laptop.

**Pain point 2:**
- Title: **Even if you got in, then what?**
- Body: Let's say you got it working. Now what? "Ask the AI to do something." Great. What? Nobody tells you what to actually DO with it for YOUR life.

**Pain point 3:**
- Title: **Nothing sticks**
- Body: You tried a tutorial. It worked once. Next day, broken. You tried an app. Used it for a week. Abandoned. The tools that promise to save time end up wasting it.

**Research card:**
- Stat: **2,847**
- Body: Posts, threads, and rants we analyzed across Reddit and forums. The same frustrations kept coming up.
- Pull quote: *The #1 answer: "I start tracking it, then I quit because it takes too long."*
- Supporting stats:
  - Email chaos -- 4 hrs/week
  - Meal planning -- 3 hrs/week
  - Social posting -- 5 hrs/week
- Tag line: Meldar picks up where you left off

**Notes:**
- "Be honest." is confrontational in the best way. It stops the scroll.
- The three pain points follow a narrative arc: tried and failed (wall) -> don't know what to do (confusion) -> nothing lasts (frustration). Each one is a mirror the reader recognizes.
- Research card is honest: "analyzed" not "asked." No implied survey methodology.

## Section 4: How It Works

**Heading:**
> How it actually works

**Subheading:**
> No installs. No permissions. No trust required upfront. Just a file you already own.

**Step 01:**
- Title: **Take back your data**
- Body: We show you exactly how to download what Google, Apple, and Meta already know about you. It takes 3 minutes. It's your legal right. Most people have no idea how much is in there.

**Step 02:**
- Title: **See your real numbers**
- Body: Drop the file into Meldar. It never leaves your device. In seconds, you see which apps eat your hours, what you search for over and over, where your week actually goes. The gap between what you think and what's real is the aha moment.

**Step 03:**
- Title: **Pick a fix. We build it.**
- Body: Your analysis shows a ranked list: "This wastes the most time. Here's what you should build to fix it." Pick the one you want. Meldar shows you how to build it yourself. Or we build it for you.

**Notes:**
- "How it actually works" has an edge of "unlike the vague promises you've seen before." Keep it.
- Step 01 reframes data download as reclamation, not compliance. "It's your legal right" adds authority.
- Step 02's "It never leaves your device" is a trust bomb buried exactly where it needs to be -- the moment of data handoff.
- Step 03 offers both paths (DIY and done-for-you) without forcing a choice. The EUR 79 build converts from here.

## Copy Principles Applied

1. **Zero jargon.** No "AI agents," no "MCP," no "API," no "CLI," no "repos" in user-facing copy. The word "GitHub" appears only in the Build tier description where it's the delivery mechanism.
2. **Every claim is true.** "2,847 posts analyzed" (real). "One person in Helsinki" (real). "Image never reaches our servers" (real, client-side OCR). No "12 engineers." No "thousands surveyed."
3. **Specificity over vagueness.** "15 minutes" not "quick call." "48 hours" not "soon." "EUR 79" not "affordable." "30 seconds" not "fast."
4. **Data reclamation as through-line.** The narrative starts in the hero ($238), builds through How It Works ("take back your data"), peaks in the Data Receipt (the visual proof), and closes in the Final CTA ("it's time it told you something useful").
5. **Voice check:** Every sentence was tested against "Would a friend who's brilliant at this say this?" If it sounds like a sales page, a developer tool, or a corporate deck, it was rewritten.
