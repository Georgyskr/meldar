# What can I do with AI to make my small business profitable?

**TL;DR:** Nine things, each doable this week, most of them free. The ones that save money are at the top. The ones that make money are at the bottom. Skip to whichever matters more to you right now.

---

I run a small company. When I search "AI for small business," I get articles written by people who clearly do not run small businesses. They say things like "leverage AI-powered insights to optimize your customer journey." I don't have a customer journey. I have a spreadsheet and a WhatsApp group.

So this is the list I wish someone had written for me. Each item is a specific thing you can do, with a specific free tool, that either saves you money or makes you money. No theory. No "10x your growth mindset." Just the list.

A note before we start: none of this requires coding. A developer could turn each of these into a polished app in a day. You, without coding, can get 80% of the value using a chat window and 20 minutes. That's the honest math, and for a small business, 80% is plenty.

## 1. Stop paying someone to sort your inbox

This is the fastest win for most small business owners, because email eats 3-4 hours a week and most of those hours are wasted on messages that don't need you.

Open Claude (free at claude.ai) or ChatGPT (free at chatgpt.com). Paste your last 20 emails. Type: "Sort these into three groups: needs my reply today, can wait until Friday, and ignore. For the ones that need a reply today, draft a two-sentence response in a friendly but professional tone."

That's it. You just did 45 minutes of triage in about 90 seconds. Save the prompt. Reuse it tomorrow. After a week you'll stop thinking of it as "using AI" and start thinking of it as "how I do email now."

Cost: $0. Time saved: 2-4 hours/week. Annual value at $50/hour: $5,000-10,000.

## 2. Automate your expense tracking

If you're still taking photos of receipts and typing numbers into a spreadsheet at month-end, you're burning time on a solved problem.

Take a photo of a receipt. Paste it into Claude. Say: "Extract the date, vendor name, amount, and tax. Categorize it as one of: supplies, meals, travel, software, or other. Return as a single CSV row."

Do this for every receipt as it comes in. At month-end, paste all the CSV rows into a spreadsheet. Done. No $15/month receipt-scanning app. No manual data entry. If you want to get fancy, ask the AI to also flag anything over $200 as "review with accountant."

Cost: $0. Replaces: $15-30/month SaaS subscription + 2 hours/month of manual entry.

## 3. Write all your social media for the week in 30 minutes

Most small business owners know they should post on social media. Most don't, because creating content for Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, and wherever else takes hours they don't have.

Pick one thing that happened in your business this week. A customer win, a problem you solved, a product you shipped. Describe it in three sentences to Claude. Then: "Write me a LinkedIn post (professional, 150 words), an Instagram caption (casual, 80 words with hashtags), and a Facebook post (conversational, 100 words). All from the same story."

Three platforms, one story, 30 seconds of your time. Do it four times and you have a week of content.

Cost: $0. Replaces: a $200-500/month social media manager or 4-5 hours/week of your own time.

## 4. Build a price monitoring tool for competitor tracking

This one saves money indirectly. If you sell anything where pricing matters (which is everything), knowing what your competitors charge, and when they change prices, is worth a lot.

You can ask ChatGPT or Claude to check competitor websites and compare pricing. For ongoing monitoring, you'll need something slightly more permanent. This is where the developer gap shows up: a developer could build a price scraper in an afternoon. Without coding, you can still do manual spot-checks weekly using AI to compile and compare the data.

The prompt: paste competitor pricing pages (or screenshots) into Claude. "Compare these prices to mine. Where am I more expensive? Where am I cheaper? Flag any competitor that's changed prices since last week." Keep a running document.

Cost: $0. Value: depends on your margins, but catching a competitor's price drop a week earlier can save a deal.

## 5. Turn customer emails into product decisions

This one is underrated. You're sitting on gold and probably don't know it.

Paste your last 50 customer emails (or support tickets, or WhatsApp messages) into Claude. Ask: "What are the top 5 things customers complain about, ask for, or praise? Rank by frequency. Quote specific messages as evidence."

I did this with three months of customer emails and learned that 40% of support requests were about the same two features. I fixed those features and support volume dropped by a third. That's money: fewer hours on support, happier customers who stick around.

You can repeat this monthly. The AI reads the emails for patterns. You decide what to do about it.

Cost: $0. Value: lower churn, fewer support hours, better product decisions.

## 6. Draft contracts, proposals, and invoices in minutes

If you spend time rewriting the same kind of document for different clients, you're doing work AI can handle.

Write your best version of a proposal or contract once. Give it to Claude as a template. Then for each new client: "Here's my proposal template. Here's the client's brief [paste]. Customize the proposal for this client. Keep the legal language identical, only change the scope, pricing, and timeline."

Same works for invoices. Same works for follow-up emails. Same works for SOWs. Every document you write more than twice should become a template the AI fills in.

Cost: $0. Time saved: 1-3 hours per client engagement.

## 7. Get a "second opinion" before spending money

This is the least glamorous item on the list and maybe the most valuable.

Before you sign up for a new tool, hire a contractor, or commit to a marketing spend, describe the decision to Claude. Include what it costs, what you expect to get, and what your alternatives are.

Not because AI gives perfect business advice. It doesn't. But it's good at identifying things you haven't considered, finding comparable options you haven't researched, and doing basic ROI math. It's a zero-cost sanity check that takes five minutes.

I've avoided at least two bad purchases this way. One was a $3,000/year analytics tool I didn't need (Claude pointed out that a free Google Analytics setup would cover 90% of what the tool offered). The other was a freelancer whose rate was 2x the market for the scope. Five minutes of asking "is this a good deal?" saved me thousands.

Cost: $0. Value: whatever you don't waste.

## 8. Build a simple FAQ tool for your website

If customers ask you the same ten questions over and over, and you're answering them manually every time, that's a leak.

Write down the ten most common questions and your best answers. Paste them into Claude. Ask it to rewrite them for your website, in a consistent tone, organized by topic. Publish them as an FAQ page.

That's the simple version. The slightly fancier version: use the same Q&A pairs to create a custom GPT (free if you have ChatGPT Plus) or a simple chat widget that answers customer questions automatically. This crosses into "a developer would do it faster" territory, but the FAQ page itself takes 20 minutes and deflects real support volume.

Cost: $0-20/month. Replaces: repetitive email and chat replies.

## 9. Generate and test marketing copy without an agency

Small businesses that can't afford a marketing agency usually end up with the same website copy they wrote in a rush three years ago. AI changes this math.

Take your current homepage. Paste it into Claude. Say: "Rewrite this homepage copy. Make it clear what we do in the first sentence, who it's for in the second sentence, and why someone should care in the third. Write three different versions so I can pick the best one."

Then: "Now write 5 Google Ads headlines (under 30 characters each) and 5 descriptions (under 90 characters) for our main service."

Then: "Write 3 subject lines for an email to past customers who haven't bought in 6 months."

You just did what a marketing agency charges $2,000-5,000/month for. Your versions won't be as polished. But they'll be 80% as good, they'll exist (which is better than the agency proposal you never signed), and you can test them for free.

Cost: $0. Replaces: the marketing work you've been putting off for two years.

## What this list is actually about

If you noticed a pattern, every item above has the same shape: take a task you do manually, describe it precisely to an AI, get a result that's 80% as good as a human specialist, and save the prompt so you can repeat it.

That's the whole game for small business AI in 2026. Not chatbots on your website. Not "AI-powered analytics dashboards." Not a $500/month tool with 47 features you'll never use. Just a chat window, your real data, and a saved prompt.

The businesses that are pulling ahead right now aren't the ones with the biggest AI budgets. They're the ones where the owner spent 20 minutes with a free tool and said "wait, it can do that?"

You don't need to be a developer to do any of this. Being a developer helps, the same way being a mechanic helps if you own a car. You don't need to be one. But if you are, or if you know one, the jump from "saved prompts" to "actual custom tools" takes about a day per tool.

For everybody else, the saved prompts are enough. Start with whichever item on this list made you think "I waste time on that." Do it once. Save the prompt. That's your first AI tool. It cost you nothing and it works forever.
