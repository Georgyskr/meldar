## 2. Display Campaign Structure — GDN Architecture

### Recommended Campaign Structure

Given the $1,500/mo budget constraint, run a lean structure:

```
Account Level
├── Campaign 1: Retargeting — Site Visitors (40% of budget = $600/mo)
│   ├── Ad Group: Quiz Abandoners (highest priority)
│   │   └── Responsive Display Ads + static 300x250, 728x90, 160x600
│   ├── Ad Group: Landing Page Visitors (non-quiz)
│   │   └── Responsive Display Ads
│   └── Frequency cap: 3x/week per user
│
├── Campaign 2: Managed Placements — Career Sites (35% of budget = $525/mo)
│   ├── Ad Group: Job Boards (Indeed, Monster, Glassdoor)
│   │   └── Responsive Display Ads + custom intent audience overlay
│   ├── Ad Group: Career Content (TheMuse, TheBalance, FlexJobs)
│   │   └── Responsive Display Ads
│   └── Frequency cap: 2x/week per user
│
└── Campaign 3: Custom Intent Prospecting (25% of budget = $375/mo)
    ├── Ad Group: "Digital Footprint" searchers
    │   └── Target people who searched "digital footprint", "what recruiters see online", "AI replacing jobs"
    ├── Ad Group: Competitor intent
    │   └── Target people who searched for resume builders, LinkedIn Premium, career coaching
    └── Frequency cap: 2x/week per user
```

### Creative Specifications
- **Responsive Display Ads**: 5 headlines, 5 descriptions, 3-5 images (1200x628 landscape, 1200x1200 square), 1-2 logos
- **Static banners** (for retargeting only, higher CTR): 300x250, 728x90, 160x600, 320x50 (mobile)
- **Messaging hierarchy**:
  - Retargeting: "You started your scan — finish it now" / "Your digital footprint score is waiting"
  - Managed placements: "What do recruiters really see when they Google you?" / "83% of hiring managers check your digital footprint"
  - Prospecting: "Free Digital Footprint Scan — see what AI knows about you"

### Bidding Strategy
- Retargeting: Target CPA ($5-8 for quiz completion)
- Managed placements: Manual CPC initially ($0.30-0.80), shift to Target CPA once 30+ conversions
- Prospecting: Maximize clicks initially, shift to Target CPA at 50+ conversions

### Key Settings
- **Device**: All devices, but bid adjust +20% mobile (career browsing skews mobile)
- **Schedule**: Weekdays 7-9am, 12-2pm, 6-10pm (commute + lunch + evening browsing)
- **Geo**: USA + UK first (largest English-speaking professional markets), expand to EU/UA with localized creative
- **Viewability target**: Use Active View targeting — bid only on 70%+ viewable inventory

## 3. ABM Strategy — Realistic Approach for a Solo-Founder Startup

### The Hard Truth on Enterprise ABM Platforms

| Platform | Annual Cost | Minimum Commitment | Verdict for Meldar |
|----------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Demandbase | $30,000-100,000/yr | 12-month contract | **No.** Completely out of budget range. |
| 6Sense | $40,000-120,000/yr | 12-month contract | **No.** Same issue. |
| Terminus | $25,000-75,000/yr | 12-month contract | **No.** |
| RollWorks | $10,000-25,000/yr | Quarterly possible | **Maybe in 6-12 months** if enterprise traction materializes. |

**The plan's mention of Demandbase/6Sense for ABM display is unrealistic at current budget/stage.** These platforms require significant investment and are designed for companies with established enterprise sales motions and ACV (annual contract value) above $10,000.

### What Meldar Should Do Instead — "Poor Man's ABM"

**Phase 1: LinkedIn-First ABM (Use existing $2,000/mo LinkedIn budget)**
- Upload a target account list (100-200 companies) as a Matched Audience in LinkedIn Campaign Manager — this is free
- Layer job title targeting: CHRO, VP People, Head of L&D, HR Director
- Run Sponsored Content + Conversation Ads specifically to this list
- This IS account-based marketing at a fraction of the cost

**Phase 2: Google Ads Customer Match + Similar Audiences**
- Upload email lists of enterprise prospects to Google Ads
- Create Customer Match audiences for display retargeting
- Build Similar Audiences from converters for prospecting
- Cost: $0 incremental (uses existing GDN budget)

**Phase 3: Manual ABM via Outbound (Week 8+ per plan)**
- Pair cold email outreach (Phase 7.2) with LinkedIn ad exposure to same accounts
- "Surround sound" effect: prospect sees LinkedIn ad, then gets cold email, then sees display retarget
- Coordinate timing: ads run 1 week before outbound email sequence starts

**Phase 4: RollWorks or Metadata.io (Only if enterprise pipeline exceeds $50K)**
- RollWorks offers lighter ABM with display ads starting at ~$800/mo
- Metadata.io automates LinkedIn + Facebook audience building for B2B
- Only worth investing when enterprise deals are in pipeline

## 4. Frequency Optimization — Cross-Platform Management

### The Problem
The plan sets a 5x/user/month cap for programmatic display, but says nothing about cross-platform frequency. A user could see:
- 5x display ads + 10x Meta ads + 5x YouTube pre-rolls + 3x LinkedIn ads + 4x TikTok = 27 impressions/month

At a $20K/mo total spend across a relatively narrow professional audience (28-45 career-focused), ad fatigue and annoyance are real risks.

### Cross-Platform Frequency Framework

| Channel | Recommended Freq Cap | Rationale |
|---------|---------------------|-----------|
| GDN Display (retargeting) | 3x/week, 10x/month | Retargeting is highest intent — slightly more exposure OK |
| GDN Display (prospecting) | 2x/week, 6x/month | Cold audience — don't burn them out |
| YouTube (pre-roll) | 1x/week, 4x/month | Video is high-attention — less frequency needed |
| Meta/Facebook | 3-5x/week (managed by Meta algo) | Let Meta's delivery optimization handle this |
| LinkedIn | 2x/week, 6x/month | Expensive — every impression must count |
| TikTok | 3-4x/week | Short-form content tolerates higher frequency |

### Total cross-platform target: 3-7 unique ad exposures per week per user across ALL channels (not per channel).

### How to Manage This in Practice
1. **Shared audience suppression lists**: Export converters weekly from each platform and upload as suppression audiences across all others. Someone who completed a paid scan should stop seeing acquisition ads everywhere.
2. **Sequential messaging**: Use platform-specific audiences to tell a story across channels:
   - First touch (display/social): "What does your digital footprint say about you?"
   - Second touch (retarget): "83% of recruiters Google you. Here's what they find."
   - Third touch (retarget): "Get your free Digital Footprint Scan — 60 seconds."
3. **Creative rotation**: Minimum 3-5 creative variants per channel, rotating every 2 weeks. The plan already calls for 20 new creatives/week for Meta — extend some of this to display and YouTube.
4. **Burnout detection**: Monitor CTR weekly. If CTR drops below 0.05% on display or view rate drops below 20% on YouTube, the audience is fatigued — rotate creative or pause and refresh targeting.

### Cross-Platform Deduplication
Without a DMP (data management platform) like LiveRamp or Oracle Data Cloud, true cross-platform deduplication is impossible. Practical workaround:
- Use Google Ads for both GDN display and YouTube — frequency is managed within the Google ecosystem
- Use Meta's cross-placement frequency for FB + IG + Reels
- LinkedIn and TikTok remain siloed — accept some overlap

## 5. Budget Reallocation — Is $1,500/mo Enough for Programmatic?

### Short Answer: No.

$1,500/mo for programmatic display is below the effective floor for the channel. Here is why:

- **Minimum viable GDN budget**: To run 3 campaigns (retarget + managed placements + prospecting) and collect enough data for automated bidding to optimize, you need 30-50 conversions/month per campaign. At an estimated $8-15 CPA, that is $720-2,250 per campaign — meaning one campaign barely fits the budget.
- **Statistical significance**: At $1,500/mo and ~$8 CPM, you get roughly 187,500 impressions. Split across 3 campaigns and multiple ad groups, no single test cell has enough volume to reach statistical significance within a reasonable timeframe.
- **YouTube at $1,500/mo**: YouTube pre-roll at $0.03-0.06 per view (CPV) yields 25,000-50,000 views/month. This is workable for a single campaign but too thin for A/B testing creative.

### Recommended Reallocation

| Channel | Current Budget | Recommended Budget | Change |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|--------|
| Meta/Facebook | $5,000-50,000 | No change | — |
| Google Search | $3,000 | $2,500 | -$500 |
| LinkedIn Ads | $2,000 | $1,500 | -$500 |
| TikTok/Reels | $2,000 | $1,500 | -$500 |
| **Programmatic Display** | **$1,500** | **$2,500** | **+$1,000** |
| **YouTube** | **$1,500** | **$2,500** | **+$1,000** |
| AI tools | $500 | $500 | — |

**Rationale for taking from Google Search, LinkedIn, TikTok:**
- **Google Search (-$500)**: Performance Max is already in the plan, which includes display/YouTube/discovery. Reducing pure search by $500 and giving it to dedicated display/YouTube gives more control over placements and creative testing.
- **LinkedIn (-$500)**: LinkedIn CPMs are $30-80. At $2,000/mo, you get 25,000-67,000 impressions — barely enough for one campaign. Cutting to $1,500 loses maybe 8,000-16,000 impressions but funds much more reach on display (187,000+ impressions with $1,000).
- **TikTok (-$500)**: Career/professional audiences are harder to target precisely on TikTok. The content-first organic approach (Phase 3 TikTok personas) will drive more value than paid for this ICP.

### Alternative: Combine Display + YouTube into one Google Ads budget
Instead of separate display and YouTube budgets, run a combined $3,000-5,000 Google ecosystem budget (Search + Display + YouTube) and let Performance Max + manual campaigns coexist. This gives Google's algorithm more flexibility to allocate spend where it performs.

## 6. CTV/OTT Opportunity Assessment

### Should Meldar Explore Connected TV?

**Not now. Revisit at $50K+/mo total ad spend.**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Minimum budget | CTV platforms (Roku, Hulu, Peacock) typically require $5,000-15,000/mo minimum |
| CPM range | $20-40 CPM for programmatic CTV |
| Targeting precision | Household-level, not individual — weaker for B2C career targeting |
| Attribution | Difficult — no click-through, must measure lift via holdout studies |
| Creative cost | 15-30s video production at broadcast quality — even with AI tools, this requires polish |
| Meldar's funnel | CTV is awareness-only — Meldar needs bottom-funnel conversions right now |

### When CTV Makes Sense for Meldar
- Monthly ad budget exceeds $50,000 and top-of-funnel channels are saturated
- Brand search volume is measurable (currently zero — no brand awareness to amplify)
- Enterprise sales motion is active and "surround sound" brand presence would support deal velocity
- Creative assets exist at broadcast quality

### What to Do Instead (Now)
- **YouTube pre-roll IS Meldar's video advertising**. It offers the same "lean-back" viewing context as CTV but with click-through attribution, lower minimum spend, and granular targeting.
- If AI-generated video quality improves enough for broadcast feel, revisit CTV via DV360 or The Trade Desk at the $50K/mo tier.

## 7. Brand Safety — Placement Exclusion Strategy

For a career/professional product, brand adjacency matters significantly. Appearing next to controversial or low-quality content destroys the trust that a "Digital Footprint Scan" product relies on.

### GDN Exclusion Categories (Enable in Campaign Settings)
- **Content labels to exclude**:
  - DL-G: Tragedy and conflict
  - DL-MA: Mature audiences
  - DL-T: Sensitive social issues
  - Parked domains
  - Error pages
  - Below-the-fold only placements
- **Sensitive categories to exclude**:
  - Crime, law enforcement, and emergencies
  - Death and tragedy
  - Military and armed conflict
  - Sexually suggestive content
  - Sensational and shocking content
  - Profanity and rough language
  - Gambling
  - Politics

### Placement Exclusion List (Add to Account-Level Exclusions)

**Site categories to exclude:**
- Made-for-advertising (MFA) sites — check against Adalytics or Jounce Media lists
- Anonymous proxy / VPN review sites (high bot traffic)
- Torrent / piracy sites
- Clickbait content farms (e.g., outbrain/taboola widget pages with no editorial oversight)
- Gaming sites (audience mismatch for career product)
- Kids/family content (COPPA risk, audience mismatch)
- Meme/humor sites (brand adjacency risk)

**Specific domains to proactively exclude:**
```
anonymous-proxy-sites.com
*.torrent*
*.pirate*
clickbait-farm-networks (add as discovered)
dailymail.co.uk (brand safety risk for professional product)
*.xxx
*.adult
```

**App exclusion:** Exclude ALL mobile app inventory initially. GDN app placements have notoriously high accidental click rates (fat-finger clicks) and near-zero conversion rates for SaaS products. This alone can save 20-40% of wasted display spend.

```
Exclude: adsenseformobileapps.com (blanket app exclusion)
Exclude: GMob app categories - all
```

### YouTube Brand Safety
- Use inventory type: **Limited inventory** (most restrictive — excludes videos with moderate+ profanity, violence, sensitive topics)
- Exclude channels: entertainment gossip, political commentary, conspiracy content
- Include only: career development, professional development, business, technology, education, AI/ML topic channels
- Use **topic targeting exclusions**: remove gaming, entertainment, beauty/fashion, sports (unless career-adjacent)

### IVT (Invalid Traffic) Monitoring
- Target: less than 3% IVT across all display campaigns
- Use Google Ads built-in invalid click reporting (free)
- If IVT exceeds 5% on any campaign, investigate placement reports and add offending sites to exclusion list
- Consider DoubleVerify or IAS verification only at $5,000+/mo display spend — cost does not justify at $1,500-2,500/mo

### Viewability Standards
- Target: 70%+ viewability on all display campaigns
- Use Active View data in Google Ads to identify low-viewability placements
- Remove any placement consistently below 50% viewability
- Above-the-fold placements preferred — bid adjust +15% for ATF when available
