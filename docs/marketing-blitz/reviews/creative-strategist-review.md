## 1. HOOK-BODY-CTA FRAMEWORK FOR MELDAR ADS

### The "Threat-Reveal-Rescue" (TRR) Framework

Meldar's product sits at the intersection of fear (career obsolescence) and curiosity (what does the internet say about me?). The optimal creative framework exploits both:

**HOOK (0-3 seconds / first line of copy)**
Pattern-interrupt that triggers either threat awareness or curiosity gap. Must be concrete, specific, and personally relevant to the viewer.

**BODY (3-15 seconds / middle copy block)**
Reveal the hidden problem with specificity. Use data points, insider language, or scenario painting. This is where you build the "information asymmetry" -- the viewer realizes they don't know something important about themselves.

**CTA (final 3 seconds / closing line)**
Low-friction action framed as self-discovery, not a purchase. Always "scan" or "check" language, never "buy" or "sign up."

### Framework in Practice:

| Element | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **HOOK** | [Shocking stat / Insider reveal / Direct challenge] | "I've rejected 200+ candidates this year. Here's the #1 reason." |
| **BODY** | [Problem specifics + what they don't know + consequence] | "Before I even read your resume, I Google your name. What comes up in the first 3 results determines if I keep reading. 83% of hiring managers do the same. Most candidates have no idea what their digital footprint actually says." |
| **CTA** | [Free + fast + self-discovery language] | "See exactly what recruiters see. Free 60-second scan." |

### CTA Hierarchy (by conversion strength):

1. "Get your free scan" -- direct, value-clear
2. "See what recruiters see about you" -- curiosity + relevance
3. "Check your Digital Footprint Score" -- gamification
4. "Find out in 60 seconds" -- speed + low commitment
5. "See your score" -- minimal friction

**Critical rule**: Never use "Learn more" or "Sign up" -- these kill CTR for curiosity-driven products. Every CTA must promise a personal revelation.

## 2. TOP 10 HOOK TEMPLATES

These hooks are ranked by predicted scroll-stop power based on the career/digital footprint vertical. Each uses a proven psychological trigger.

### Tier A -- Highest Predicted CTR (use for cold audiences)

**1. The Insider Confession**
> "As a recruiter, I'm going to tell you something most candidates never hear..."
- Trigger: Authority + exclusivity
- Best for: Video (UGC-style), persona page posts
- Why it works: Positions the viewer as getting privileged information

**2. The Hidden Threat**
> "Your LinkedIn profile is costing you interviews. Here's what you can't see."
- Trigger: Loss aversion + curiosity gap
- Best for: Static image, carousel first card
- Why it works: Makes a familiar platform suddenly threatening

**3. The Stat Bomb**
> "83% of recruiters Google you before calling. Here's what they find."
- Trigger: Social proof of threat + fear of unknown
- Best for: All formats, especially video overlay text
- Why it works: Concrete number + personal stakes

**4. The Direct Challenge**
> "Google yourself right now. I'll wait."
- Trigger: Interactive dare + immediate relevance
- Best for: Video, Reels/TikTok
- Why it works: Triggers immediate action impulse; even those who don't Google become curious

**5. The Contrast Reveal**
> "What your resume says about you vs. what Google says about you"
- Trigger: Duality / expectation violation
- Best for: Carousel, split-image static
- Why it works: Implies a gap the viewer hasn't considered

### Tier B -- Strong CTR (use for warm/retargeting audiences)

**6. The Time Bomb**
> "That blog post from 2019 is still the first thing employers see about you."
- Trigger: Forgotten past + present consequence
- Best for: Static image with "search result" mockup
- Why it works: Triggers a specific memory + immediate dread

**7. The Comparison Trap**
> "Your competitors for that role have already optimized their digital footprint. Have you?"
- Trigger: Competitive anxiety + FOMO
- Best for: Carousel, LinkedIn Sponsored Content
- Why it works: Directly relevant to active job seekers

**8. The AI Scare**
> "AI hiring tools now scan your entire online presence in 3 seconds. Here's what they flag."
- Trigger: Technology threat + helplessness
- Best for: Video with AI visual effects, Reels
- Why it works: Combines two anxieties (AI + surveillance) into one

**9. The Authority Warning**
> "I've hired 500+ people. The ones who got rejected never knew the real reason."
- Trigger: Injustice + curiosity
- Best for: UGC video from recruiter persona
- Why it works: Implies a systemic problem the viewer is victim to

**10. The Score Tease**
> "Your Digital Footprint Score is probably lower than you think. Most people score under 40/100."
- Trigger: Gamification + ego challenge
- Best for: Static with score graphic, retargeting
- Why it works: Creates compulsion to prove the ad wrong

### Hook Usage Rules:
- Rotate no more than 3 hooks per persona page at a time
- Retire any hook below 1.5% CTR after 1,000 impressions
- A/B test Tier A hooks first; move to Tier B for retargeting and warmer audiences
- Localize hooks for UK/EU (e.g., "CV" not "resume", "hiring managers" not "recruiters" where appropriate)

## 3. AD FORMAT RECOMMENDATIONS BY PLATFORM

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

| Format | Priority | Rationale | Specs |
|--------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **UGC-style vertical video (Reels)** | #1 | Lowest CPM, highest engagement, fits persona page strategy. AI-generated "recruiter talking to camera" with subtitles. | 9:16, 15-30s, captions mandatory |
| **Carousel** | #2 | Great for "5 things recruiters see" educational content. Each card reveals one element. Drives swipe engagement (positive signal to algo). | 1:1, 3-5 cards, text overlay on each |
| **Single image with text overlay** | #3 | Fast to produce, easy to A/B test headlines. Use "search result" mockups or "score card" visuals. | 1:1 or 4:5, <20% text rule relaxed but keep legible |

**Key Meta insight**: The plan's 250 concurrent campaigns are aggressive. Algorithmic learning requires ~50 conversions/week per ad set to exit learning phase. With $5K starting budget spread across 250 campaigns, each gets ~$20/month -- far too thin. **Recommendation: Start with 10-15 campaigns across 5 pages, 3 ad sets each, concentrate budget for faster optimization signals.**

## 4. CREATIVE TESTING ROADMAP -- FIRST 8 WEEKS

### Week 1-2: Foundation Tests

**Goal**: Identify winning hook type and primary format.

| Test | Variable | Control | Variant(s) | Platform | Budget | Min Sample |
|------|----------|---------|------------|----------|--------|------------|
| Hook Type | Hook category | Insider Confession hook | Hidden Threat / Stat Bomb / Direct Challenge | Meta | $1,500 | 5,000 impressions per variant |
| Format | Ad format | UGC video | Carousel / Single image | Meta | $1,000 | 3,000 impressions per |
| CTA Language | CTA text | "Get your free scan" | "See what recruiters see" / "Check your score" | Meta | $500 | 2,000 clicks per |

**Decision criteria**: Primary metric = CTR (click-through rate). Secondary = quiz start rate. Kill any variant below 50% of control performance after minimum sample reached.

### Week 3-4: Body & Messaging Tests

**Goal**: Optimize the message between hook and CTA.

| Test | Variable | Control | Variant(s) | Platform |
|------|----------|---------|------------|----------|
| Pain Point | Core fear | Career obsolescence (AI replacing jobs) | Digital invisibility (recruiters can't find you) / Hidden reputation damage | Meta + Google |
| Proof Type | Credibility element | Statistic ("83% of recruiters...") | Persona authority ("As a recruiter with 15 years...") / User testimonial | Meta |
| Urgency | Time pressure | None | "Your footprint is being checked right now" / "Before your next interview" | Meta |

**Decision criteria**: Primary = quiz completion rate (not just CTR -- deeper funnel matters now). Secondary = CPA.

### Week 5-6: Persona & Audience Tests

**Goal**: Identify which persona types and audience segments perform best.

| Test | Variable | Control | Variant(s) | Platform |
|------|----------|---------|------------|----------|
| Persona Type | Who delivers the message | Recruiter persona | Career coach / HR insider / Tech lead | Meta |
| Audience Segment | Targeting | Broad professionals 28-45 | Job title targeting / Interest: career development / Life event: new job | Meta |
| Geo | Market | USA | UK / EU (English) / Ukraine (localized) | Meta + Google |
| Platform | Channel | Meta (winning creative) | Same creative on TikTok / YouTube / Google Discovery | Cross-platform |

**Decision criteria**: Primary = CPA for quiz completion. Secondary = free-to-paid conversion rate (attribution window: 7 days).

### Week 7-8: Scale & Iteration Tests

**Goal**: Squeeze more performance from winners; test creative refresh concepts.

| Test | Variable | Control | Variant(s) |
|------|----------|---------|------------|
| Creative Refresh | New vs. fatigued | Winning creative from wk 1-4 | Same hook, new visuals / New hook, same visual structure / Completely new concept |
| Landing Page Match | LP alignment | Generic LP | Hook-matched LP (headline mirrors ad hook exactly) |
| Retargeting Creative | Retargeting message | Same ad re-served | Objection handling ("Still not sure? Here's what you're missing") / Social proof ("Join 2,000+ professionals") / Urgency ("Score expires in 48 hours") |
| Video Length | Duration | 15s | 30s / 45s / 60s |

**Decision criteria**: Primary = ROAS (factor in paid conversion, not just quiz). Secondary = creative fatigue rate (frequency vs. CTR decay).

### Statistical Rigor Rules (All Tests):
- Minimum 95% confidence interval before declaring a winner
- No test runs less than 7 days (account for day-of-week variance)
- Minimum 100 conversions per variant for CPA-based decisions
- Document every test in a shared spreadsheet: hypothesis, variants, sample size, result, decision, learning
- Only test ONE variable at a time (multivariate testing requires 10x the traffic you'll have in early weeks)

## 5. AI CREATIVE PRODUCTION SOP -- 20 CREATIVES/WEEK

### Weekly Production Calendar

| Day | Activity | Output |
|-----|----------|--------|
| **Monday** | Review prior week's performance data. Identify top 3 performers and bottom 3. Generate new creative briefs based on learnings. | 5 creative briefs |
| **Tuesday** | Copy production: Feed briefs into ChatGPT/Claude with Meldar prompt library. Generate 5 copy variants per brief. Select top 2 per brief. | 10 copy variants finalized |
| **Wednesday** | Visual production: Midjourney for static images (headshots, lifestyle, transformation, search result mockups). Generate 3-4 visual variants per brief. | 15-20 visual assets |
| **Thursday** | Video production: Runway for motion graphics, HeyGen for talking-head persona videos, ElevenLabs for voiceover. Assemble video creatives. | 8-10 video creatives |
| **Friday** | Assembly + QA: Combine copy + visuals into final ad units. Format for each platform (aspect ratios, text overlays, captions). Upload to ad accounts. Schedule launches for Monday. | 20+ final creatives uploaded |

### Prompt Library Structure

Maintain a versioned prompt library organized by:

**Copy prompts** (ChatGPT/Claude):
```
ROLE: You are a [persona type: recruiter/career coach/HR insider] with [X] years of experience.
PRODUCT: Meldar Digital Footprint Scan -- free tool that shows professionals what recruiters see online.
HOOK TYPE: [Insider Confession / Hidden Threat / Stat Bomb / Direct Challenge / etc.]
FORMAT: [Single image caption / Carousel card text / Video script / RSA headline]
TONE: Conversational, authoritative, slightly alarming but empowering.
CONSTRAINTS: Hook must be under 10 words. Body max 3 sentences. CTA must include "free" and action verb.
OUTPUT: 5 variants ranked by predicted scroll-stop power.
```

**Visual prompts** (Midjourney):
```
Category 1 -- Professional headshots: "Professional headshot, [ethnicity], [age range], [gender], corporate attire, soft studio lighting, warm confident expression, LinkedIn-style, 4:5 aspect ratio --v 6"

Category 2 -- Transformation: "Split image, left side: stressed professional at desk, dim lighting, cluttered papers. Right side: same person, confident, bright modern office, smiling. Career transformation concept --ar 1:1 --v 6"

Category 3 -- Search result mockup: "Realistic Google search results page showing a person's name, professional photo, career information, clean UI --ar 16:9 --v 6"

Category 4 -- Data visualization: "Futuristic holographic display showing a person's digital footprint score, glowing blue metrics, professional setting --ar 4:5 --v 6"
```

**Video prompts** (Runway/HeyGen):
```
Scene: [persona type] sitting in professional setting, looking directly at camera
Script: [paste finalized copy from Tuesday]
Tone: Natural, conversational, slightly concerned expression transitioning to confident
Duration: [15s/30s]
Captions: Large, centered, white text with dark background bar
```

### Quality Control Checklist (Every Creative)

- [ ] Hook is visible/audible within first 2 seconds
- [ ] No text below the fold on mobile (test 9:16 viewport)
- [ ] CTA is clear and contains action verb
- [ ] Brand colors/logo present but not dominant (brand awareness is secondary to conversion)
- [ ] AI-generated faces pass the "uncanny valley" check (zoom to 200%, check eyes/hands/teeth)
- [ ] Copy contains no claims that could trigger ad policy rejection (no guaranteed results, no medical/financial claims)
- [ ] All text is legible at mobile scale (minimum 24pt equivalent)
- [ ] Video has captions (85% of social video watched without sound)
- [ ] Platform-specific specs met (aspect ratio, file size, duration limits)
- [ ] UTM parameters correctly appended to destination URL

### Batch Efficiency Tips

- Generate 20+ Midjourney images in a single session, curate the best 10
- Use ChatGPT/Claude "continue" pattern to generate all 5 briefs' copy in one session
- Template video editing in CapCut or DaVinci Resolve: create 3-4 master templates, swap assets weekly
- Build a Notion/Airtable creative database: tag every asset by hook type, format, persona, platform, test status, performance

## 6. FATIGUE MANAGEMENT

### How Creative Fatigue Works

Creative fatigue occurs when the same audience sees the same ad too many times, causing CTR to decline and CPA to increase. For Meldar's volume strategy (250 campaigns, 20 new creatives/week), fatigue management is critical.

### Fatigue Detection System

**Leading Indicators** (detect early):
| Metric | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Frequency | > 2.5 in 7 days | Rotate creative or expand audience |
| CTR day-over-day decline | > 15% decline for 3 consecutive days | Pause and replace creative |
| CPM increase | > 20% increase at same targeting | Creative or audience saturated |
| Thumb-stop rate (video) | < 25% after previously > 40% | Hook has lost novelty |

**Lagging Indicators** (confirm fatigue):
| Metric | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| CPA increase | > 30% above best-performing period | Immediate creative refresh |
| Negative feedback rate | > 0.5% hide/report | Pull creative immediately |
| Conversion rate decline | > 20% decline from peak | Full creative concept refresh needed |

### Fatigue Prevention Tactics

**1. Creative Rotation Calendar**
- Every creative has a maximum lifespan of 21 days in active rotation
- After 21 days: pause regardless of performance, rest for 14 days, then retest
- High performers can be "reborn" with visual refresh (new colors, new imagery, same copy) after rest period

**2. Audience Rotation**
- Don't just rotate creatives -- rotate audiences
- When a creative fatigues on Audience A, test it on Audience B before retiring
- Use exclusion lists to prevent cross-audience contamination

**3. The 70/20/10 Rule for Weekly Creative Mix**
- 70% of budget: Proven winners (current best performers)
- 20% of budget: Iterative variants (same structure, tweaked elements)
- 10% of budget: Wild cards (completely new concepts, untested hooks)

**4. Hook Cycling Strategy**
Maintain a "hook vault" of 20+ tested hooks. Cycle through them on a 3-week rotation:
- Week 1-3: Hooks A, B, C active
- Week 4-6: Hooks D, E, F active (A, B, C resting)
- Week 7-9: Hooks A, B, C return with new visuals

**5. Persona Page Advantage**
The multi-page strategy actually helps with fatigue -- the same audience can see similar messages from different "people" without it registering as repetition. Ensure each persona page uses distinct visual identity (different headshot, color palette, name) even when running the same hook framework.

**6. Platform-Specific Fatigue Rates**
| Platform | Typical fatigue onset | Refresh frequency |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Meta (feed) | 10-14 days | Weekly refresh of bottom 30% |
| Meta (Reels) | 7-10 days | 2x/week refresh |
| TikTok | 5-7 days | 3x/week refresh |
| Google Search | 30-60 days | Monthly review |
| YouTube | 14-21 days | Bi-weekly refresh |
| LinkedIn | 21-30 days | Bi-weekly refresh |
| Display | 7-14 days | Weekly refresh |

## 7. PERSONA-SPECIFIC CREATIVE VARIATIONS

The plan identifies three primary persona types for the page farm: career coaches, recruiters, and hiring managers. Each requires distinct creative treatment.

### Persona 1: The Recruiter

**Voice**: Direct, slightly blunt, insider-knowledge tone. "I see this every day and most people have no idea."

**Hook style**: Confession/insider reveal
- "I rejected 3 candidates today before reading their resumes."
- "The first thing I do when I get your application? Google your name."
- "Want to know why you didn't get the callback? It wasn't your resume."

**Visual style**: Professional but approachable. Business casual, modern office, laptop open. Slightly younger demographic (30-40) to feel relatable.

**Body messaging**: Focus on what happens behind the scenes in hiring. Reveal the invisible process. Make the viewer feel like they're getting access to a secret system.

**CTA framing**: "See what I see when I search your name."

**Best platforms**: Meta (video + carousel), TikTok (talking head), YouTube (pre-roll)

---

### Persona 2: The Career Coach

**Voice**: Empathetic, motivational, solution-oriented. "I've helped hundreds of professionals transform their careers, and there's one thing most of them overlook."

**Hook style**: Mentor guidance / gentle warning
- "If you're job hunting and haven't checked your digital footprint, we need to talk."
- "The #1 career mistake I see professionals over 30 making."
- "Your skills are incredible. But here's why companies aren't calling."

**Visual style**: Warm, aspirational. Well-lit home office or coffee shop. Approachable wardrobe. Slightly older (35-50) to convey experience and wisdom.

**Body messaging**: Focus on transformation and empowerment. Less fear, more opportunity. "You deserve to be seen the way you really are." Position the scan as a coaching tool, not a threat detector.

**CTA framing**: "Discover your Digital Footprint Score -- it's the first step to getting noticed."

**Best platforms**: Meta (carousel, long-form video), LinkedIn (Sponsored Content), Instagram (Reels with coaching tips)

---

### Persona 3: The Hiring Manager / HR Insider

**Voice**: Authoritative, corporate, data-driven. "At our company, we have a standard process before we interview anyone."

**Hook style**: Corporate process reveal / systemic exposure
- "Every candidate at our company goes through a digital footprint check. Here's what disqualifies you."
- "I manage hiring for a 500-person company. Here's what our HR software flags about you."
- "Your digital footprint is scored before you even walk into the interview."

**Visual style**: Corporate, polished. Conference room, enterprise office, business attire. Gender-diverse, 35-55 age range. Authority signals (name badge, branded background).

**Body messaging**: Focus on systems and processes. Make the viewer realize this isn't one recruiter's opinion -- it's an automated, company-wide practice. The threat is institutional, not personal.

**CTA framing**: "Find out what hiring systems flag about you. Free scan."

**Best platforms**: LinkedIn (primary -- this persona resonates most with LinkedIn's audience), Meta (for retargeting), Google Discovery

---

### Persona Deployment Matrix

| Persona | Cold Audience | Warm/Retarget | Platform Priority |
|---------|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Recruiter | Primary -- highest scroll-stop power for cold traffic | Secondary | Meta, TikTok, YouTube |
| Career Coach | Secondary -- warm but lower urgency | Primary -- nurture and convert | Meta, Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Hiring Manager | Tertiary -- too corporate for cold social | Primary for B2B | LinkedIn, Google, Display |

### Cross-Persona Rules

1. **Never mix personas within the same ad set** -- audience should only see one persona type per ad set to maintain consistency
2. **Use retargeting to escalate personas**: Cold audience sees Recruiter (alarm) --> Retarget with Career Coach (solution) --> Retarget with Hiring Manager (validation)
3. **Each persona page must have a consistent visual identity**: same headshot, color accent, name, and bio across all creatives from that page
4. **Localization**: Recruiter and Career Coach personas work globally. Hiring Manager persona needs market-specific adaptation (employment law references, job market data)

## CRITICAL GAPS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### Gap 1: Budget vs. Campaign Volume Mismatch
The plan proposes 250 concurrent Meta campaigns on a $5K starting budget. That's $20/month per campaign -- nowhere near enough data for algorithmic optimization. **Fix: Start with 15-20 campaigns across 5 pages. Each campaign needs $250-500/month minimum to exit Meta's learning phase. Scale pages as budget scales.**

### Gap 2: No Creative Scoring System
The plan lacks a standardized way to grade creatives before and after launch. **Fix: Implement a pre-launch "Creative Score" (hook strength, CTA clarity, visual quality, platform fit -- each scored 1-5) and a post-launch performance grade (CTR vs. benchmark, CPA vs. target, fatigue rate).**

### Gap 3: No Ad Policy Risk Assessment
AI-generated faces, fake testimonials, and fabricated media logos carry significant ad policy risk on Meta, Google, and LinkedIn. Meta's AI-generated content policy (updated 2025) requires disclosure. Fake testimonials violate FTC guidelines and platform ToS. **Fix: Add "Paid partnership" or "AI-generated" labels where required. Replace fake testimonials with anonymized real user quotes as soon as possible. Remove fake "As seen in" logos immediately -- this alone can get your ad account permanently banned.**

### Gap 4: No Creative Asset Management System
20 creatives/week across multiple personas, platforms, and test variants will create asset chaos within 4 weeks. **Fix: Set up Airtable or Notion database from Day 1 with fields: creative ID, hook type, persona, format, platform, test status, launch date, performance tier, retirement date.**

### Gap 5: Video Production Quality Threshold
AI-generated talking-head videos from HeyGen/Runway are improving but still carry uncanny-valley risk, especially at scale. A single obviously-fake video going viral for the wrong reasons could damage brand trust irreparably. **Fix: Quality-gate every video with a 3-person review (even if those 3 people are the same solo founder at different times of day). Any video that feels "off" gets killed. Consider supplementing with real face-on-camera content from the founder for highest-stakes placements.**

### Gap 6: Missing Frequency Capping Strategy
The plan mentions 5x/user/month for display but has no frequency strategy for Meta, TikTok, or YouTube. **Fix: Set frequency caps -- Meta: 3x/user/week per ad set; TikTok: 4x/user/week; YouTube: 2x/user/week for non-skippable, unlimited for skippable.**
