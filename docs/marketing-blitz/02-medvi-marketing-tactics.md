# MedVi Marketing Tactics — Granular Breakdown

Last updated: 2026-04-13

Source: Futurism investigation, FDA warning letter, court filings, Meta Ad Library, web research.

---

## 1. Facebook/Meta Ad Strategy (Primary Growth Lever)

100% of acquisition was paid. No SEO, no content, no organic social.

### The Page Farm Model

- 800+ standalone Facebook pages, each posing as an individual physician
- AI-generated profile photos (Midjourney, some with visible Gemini watermarks)
- Names from plausible ("Dr. Daniel Foster, MD") to absurd ("Professor Albust Dongledore")
- Categorized under "Entertainment website" to avoid health-content moderation
- Non-existent physical addresses

### Page Growth Playbook

1. Create page with AI headshot, fake name, fake bio
2. $500-1,000 per page in follow campaigns
3. Target US health/wellness interests at ~$0.01/follow
4. Reach 5K-10K followers per page
5. Seed 10-15 organic posts to build page history
6. Revenue-sharing on Reels as dual income

### Ad Campaign Scale

- 5,000+ simultaneous active ad campaigns at peak
- Dropped to ~2,800 after Business Insider coverage
- ~30% through affiliates, ~70% direct

### Creative Production

- Midjourney for images (product shots, lifestyle, before/after)
- Runway for video (testimonial-style, transformation stories)
- ElevenLabs for voiceover
- ChatGPT/Claude for ad copy at volume
- UGC-style "doctor recommendation" format performed best
- Real weight-loss photos from Reddit/Daily Mail with AI face-swaps

### Targeting

- Women 35-55 in Facebook health/wellness/weight management groups
- Demographic least likely to detect AI-generated content
- Lookalike audiences from converters

### Why Facebook Specifically

1. Only platform that lets you create unlimited pages that look like real people
2. Target demo (women 35-55) IS Facebook's core audience
3. Cheapest CPMs ($5-15) for this demographic
4. Weakest moderation on health claims (reactive, not proactive)
5. Organic + paid hybrid on same entity
6. Consensus illusion: same user sees 3-5 "different doctors" recommending same thing

## 2. Fake Social Proof

### Fake Media Logos
- Landing page displayed NYT, Bloomberg, Forbes logos
- Coverage did NOT exist at launch — logos were decorative trust signals

### Fake Testimonials
- AI-generated patient stories with names ("Sandra K.", "Michael P.")
- Real transformation photos sourced from Reddit/Daily Mail, AI face-swapped
- Video testimonials generated with AI voice + stock footage

### Review Manipulation
- 11,400+ Trustpilot reviews at 4.4/5 (suspicious volume for company age)
- Affiliate review sites showed 4.94/5
- BBB F-rating

### Fake Doctor Profiles
- 800+ AI-generated profiles on Facebook
- Also used real doctors' names/photos without consent (removed after reporter inquiries)

## 3. The Funnel

### Landing Page
- Hero image (AI-generated)
- Bold promise ("Lose 40 pounds by July")
- Fake media logos
- Patient testimonials
- "Starting under $200"
- CTA: "Check your eligibility"

### Quiz Funnel (~3 minutes)
- Personal info, medical history, goals
- Displays "94% Success Probability" (hardcoded — identical for everyone)
- Immediate pricing presentation
- Async physician review (rubber stamp)
- Auto-enrollment in subscription

### Pricing
- Semaglutide injections: $179 first month, $299/mo ongoing
- Tirzepatide: $349/mo
- Oral tablets: $249/mo
- Cash-pay only (no insurance)
- First-month anchor significantly below ongoing rate

---

## 4. Retention Through Friction

- Auto-renewal by default
- Cancellation requires 72+ hours notice via email (not self-serve)
- Published policy: "IN NO EVENT SHALL YOU BE ISSUED A REFUND"
- Money-back guarantee only after 5 months adherence, minus 25% fee
- Terms reportedly changed post-enrollment

## 5. Affiliate & Spam Infrastructure

### Affiliate Partner
- "The Offer Inc." (Canadian) was primary acquisition partner
- Generated "tens of millions per month" for MedVi
- Per-acquisition fee model with tracking pixels and custom landing pages
- Relationship collapsed (now suing MedVi for $1.07M)

### Spam Tactics
- Unsolicited SMS to Do Not Call Registry numbers
- Mass email from spoofed domains (sender: "147936572@kcjvjydhd.us")
- Falsified opt-in claims
- Result: 4 federal lawsuits + 1 state court case

## 6. Product Expansion as Cross-Sell

- GLP-1 weight loss (Sept 2024, 250K customers)
- ED medications (Feb 2026, 50K customers in first month)
- Planned: women's health, supplements, skincare, meal delivery
- Shared intake infrastructure enables cheap cross-sell

## 7. AI Operations (The Margin Advantage)

MedVi's 16.2% net margin vs Hims' 5.5% came from:
- AI customer service (replaced 1,000+ daily calls)
- AI creative production (no design team)
- AI code (entire platform built by non-engineer)
- AI agents connecting CRM + analytics + customer service
- Revenue per employee: ~$900M (MedVi) vs ~$1M (Hims)
