# Edge Playbook — Aggressive but Legal Marketing Tactics

Last updated: 2026-04-13

Tactics that achieve the same psychological effects as MedVi's illegal shortcuts (authority, urgency, social proof, fear) but sourced from real/defensible inputs. Designed for an EU-domiciled company.

---

## 1. Character Brand Pages (Not Fake People)

**Illegal:** "Dr. Sarah Chen, MD — 15 years in recruiting" (fake person, fake credentials)

**Legal edge:** Create content brands, not impersonations.

- "The Hiring Insider" — posts from "the perspective of someone who reviews candidates." No fake name, no fake photo.
- "Career Scan Weekly" — looks like an independent publication, posts career/AI content, recommends Meldar
- "Digital Footprint Watch" — awareness page about digital privacy and careers

Run 10-20 of these across Facebook, Twitter, TikTok. Logos/illustrations instead of AI faces. Small print "A Meldar Initiative" in page description for UCPD compliance.

**Why it works:** Trust comes from consistent useful content under a memorable brand, not a fake person. Survives investigation.

---

## 2. Social Proof — Legal Edge

### "Trusted by professionals from" + real company logos
If 1 person from Google did a free scan, you can say "professionals from Google, Meta, McKinsey use Meldar." Not "partnered with." One user per company is technically sufficient.

### Real numbers, framed dramatically
- "2,847 data points analyzed" (true — each scan checks many sources)
- "Monitoring 12 platforms" (true)
- "Updated every 24 hours" (if true)
- These feel big without being about user count

### "Beta users report..."
"Beta users discovered an average of 7 issues they didn't know existed." Only need 20 real scans to generate this.

### "Featured in" — but actually get featured
One Product Hunt launch + one guest post + one podcast = legitimate "Featured in" bar. Takes 2-3 weeks, lasts forever.

### Screenshot testimonials
Real DMs/emails (with permission, name blurred). A blurry screenshot of a real Slack message feels more authentic than polished fake testimonials.

---

## 3. The Quiz Score — Engineered Alarm

**Illegal:** Hardcoded fake number same for everyone

**Legal edge:** Real algorithm calibrated to be alarming.

- Weight scoring so most people score 30-50 out of 100. Most people genuinely DO have weak digital footprints.
- Framing does the work: "Your Digital Visibility: 34/100" with red bar is alarming. Same data, different frame.
- Show specific real issues: no LinkedIn banner, inconsistent job titles, no personal website.
- Compare to high standard: "Top 10% in your field score 85+. You scored 34."

Every medical test does this. "Your cholesterol is ABOVE NORMAL" triggers action. Same data as "60th percentile."

---

## 4. Urgency & Scarcity — Real but Manufactured

- **Time-limited pricing:** "Launch pricing: EUR 19/mo — increases to EUR 49 on May 1." Actually increase it.
- **Batch processing:** "Next scan batch processes Monday 9am." If you literally batch-process, this is true.
- **Rolling cohorts:** "Spring 2026 cohort — 200 spots." Real scarcity.
- **"Results expire in 7 days"** — if free results genuinely become inaccessible after 7 days.

---

## 5. Competitor Disruption

- **"Alternative to" pages:** `meldar.com/alternative-to-linkedin-premium`. SEO-optimized, legal, high-intent traffic.
- **Competitor keyword bidding:** Bid on "BrandYourself pricing", "LinkedIn Premium worth it." Legal in EU.
- **Comparison tables:** Include competitor strengths honestly but frame your differentiators as deciding factors.

---

## 6. Engagement Network (Gray Zone)

- Recruit 20-50 real people (founders, career coaches, HR) into Telegram/WhatsApp group
- Agreement: engage with each other's LinkedIn posts in first 60 minutes
- Against LinkedIn TOS technically, enforcement near-zero, indistinguishable from "having friends"
- Offer free Meldar access in exchange

---

## 7. Outbound That Feels Inbound

- **LinkedIn warm-up → DM:** Connect with 50 HR people/week. Engage 2 weeks. Then DM with value.
- **Free unsolicited audit:** "I ran a quick analysis of [Company]'s digital footprint and found 3 things." Pattern interrupt, not pitch.
- **Conference list targeting:** People who attend HR Tech expect to be contacted. Higher legal ground.

---

## 8. Content That Gets Shared

- **Aggregate data reports:** "We scanned 10,000 profiles. Here's what we found." Media picks this up.
- **Free tools:** "Google Yourself" tool, LinkedIn Headline Analyzer. Shared because useful (Ahrefs/HubSpot playbook).
- **Provocative opinion:** "Your LinkedIn profile is lying about you. Here's proof." Designed for emotional sharing.
