## Why They're Still Operating (7 Structural Reasons)

1. **Regulatory machinery is slow.** FDA letters are "informal and advisory." Escalation takes months to years.
2. **Revenue buys legal defense.** At $401M revenue, they can fight every case.
3. **Affiliate liability shield.** MedVi blames "affiliates" and "third-party agencies" for worst practices. Untested but not yet pierced (except in contract dispute).
4. **Meta profits from the spend.** ~10% of Meta's 2024 revenue came from questionable ads. Financial incentives not to ban high spenders.
5. **Industry-wide problem.** 30+ telehealth companies got similar FDA warnings. Regulators spread thin.
6. **US jurisdiction.** Complaint-driven, slow, resource-constrained enforcement.
7. **LegitScript certification still active.** Enables payment processing and advertising.

## EU-Specific Legal Risks

| Tactic | EU Law | Consequence |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Fake "As seen in" logos | UCPD Annex I #4 (blacklisted) | No defense possible. Fines to 4% annual turnover or EUR 2M |
| AI-generated fake testimonials | UCPD + EU AI Act Art. 50(2) | Must disclose AI content. Fines applicable |
| Fake reviews/seeding | UCPD Annex I #23b (since 2022) | Explicitly blacklisted since Omnibus Directive |
| Fake case studies | UCPD Art. 6(1)(b) | Misleading claims about results |

**UCPD follows the trader's domicile.** Finnish Consumer Ombudsman can investigate a Finnish company's deceptive practices regardless of target market.
