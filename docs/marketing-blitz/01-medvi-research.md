# MedVi Research — Solopreneur Case Study

Last updated: 2026-04-13

Source: Web research conducted 2026-04-12. NYT, Futurism, FDA, court filings.

---

## What is MedVi

DTC telehealth platform selling compounded GLP-1 weight-loss meds (semaglutide, tirzepatide) at $179-$399/month vs $1,000+/month for branded Ozempic/Wegovy.

## Who Built It

**Matthew Gallagher**, 41, self-taught programmer from LA. No degree. Previously built **Watch Gang** (watch subscription, $11M revenue) but failed with 60 employees and zero profit. That failure made him obsess over staying lean.

Brother **Elliot** (36) is the only other employee. Everything else outsourced.

## How He Built It

- **AI-first build**: ChatGPT, Claude, Grok for code/copy. Midjourney + Runway for creative. ElevenLabs for voice.
- **Zero clinical infra**: Outsourced everything medical to CareValidate and OpenLoop Health (clinicians, pharmacy, shipping, compliance)
- **Lean vendor stack**: 7 contract account managers, external law firm, accounting firm, media agencies
- The "2-person company" is really a 2-person orchestration layer on top of a vendor ecosystem

## What Actually Drove Success

1. **Market timing** — exploited a massive pricing arbitrage during peak GLP-1 demand
2. **Extreme outsourcing** — built a marketing company, not a healthcare company
3. **AI as productivity multiplier** — functional platform in 2 months for $20K
4. **Discipline from failure** — Watch Gang's bloated headcount taught him to stay lean
5. **Speed of execution** — first-mover advantage in the compounded GLP-1 window

## The Dark Side

- **FDA Warning Letter** (Feb 2026) — misleading marketing, false claims
- **5 active lawsuits** (4 federal, 1 state) — spam, TCPA, RICO, breach of contract
- **Data breach** at clinical partner OpenLoop — 1.6M patient records exposed
- **Fake doctor marketing** (800+ AI-generated Facebook personas) — exposed by Futurism
- **Moat closing** — Novo Nordisk launched FDA-approved Wegovy at $149/month (Mar 31, 2026)

## Key Lesson

MedVi is a **marketing arbitrage play** on compounded GLP-1 pricing, not an "AI company." The strategy (aggressive paid acquisition, outsource everything, move fast) is sound. The illegal shortcuts (fake doctors, fake testimonials, spam) only work if you're printing money fast enough to outrun the consequences.
