# Meldar Hero Section — Design Brief for Stitch AI

## What this is

The hero section of Meldar's landing page. This is the FIRST thing anyone sees. It needs to be mind-blowing. Not "nice website" mind-blowing. "I need to send this to everyone I know" mind-blowing.

## What Meldar is

Meldar helps non-technical people finally use AI. Three problems, three solutions:

1. "I want to use AI but don't know how" → We walk you in, hand by hand
2. "I want my own app but don't know what to build" → We analyze your daily patterns and show you what's worth building
3. "I want it to last, not break after a week" → We set you up right so it stays stable

The audience is 18-28 year olds who use ChatGPT daily but can't go deeper. They're frustrated, not stupid. Every AI tool they tried was too complex.

## The structure (must fit in one viewport, no scrolling)

Two zones stacked:

**Top zone** (takes most of the space, vertically centered):
- Small label: "For people who want AI to work — not to work on AI"
- Big headline: "You don't need to become technical. You need Meldar." (the "You need Meldar" part should have a gradient accent)
- Email capture: input field + "Get your free time audit" button
- Microcopy: "Free forever. No credit card."

**Bottom zone** (anchored to the bottom, horizontal strip):
- Three cards side by side, separated by subtle dividers
- Each card has: an icon, the pain point in quotes ("I want to use AI" *but don't know how*), and one short line explaining how Meldar solves it
- This strip should feel like a foundation the hero rests on

## The three cards

Card 1:
- Pain: "I want to use AI" *but don't know how*
- Fix: We walk you in. Hand by hand. Working AI in 30 minutes.

Card 2:
- Pain: "I want my own app" *but don't know what to build*
- Fix: We analyze your patterns and show you what's worth building.

Card 3:
- Pain: "I want it to last" *not break after a week*
- Fix: We set you up right from a quick interview. It stays stable.

## Brand values

- Confident but warm. Like a friend who's brilliant at this stuff.
- Direct. Short sentences. No fluff.
- Personal. This is YOUR thing. Nobody else's.
- Non-technical. Zero jargon. Just human words.
- Young energy. For people on TikTok and Instagram.

## Design references

- **Leonardo.ai** — bold typography that commands the viewport, confident premium energy
- **Lightspark** — warm off-white base, extreme restraint, generous whitespace, credibility through simplicity
- **spark.fi** — warm gradient accents, oversized ghost text as decoration, editorial type scale
- **ClickTheRoad** — custom illustrations that are the brand, warm approachable energy

## What makes this hero mind-blowing

- The three pain points should feel like someone reading your mind. The visitor should think "that's literally me" within 2 seconds.
- The typography should COMMAND attention. Not whisper. Not shout. Command.
- The gradient accent should feel like energy, not decoration.
- The bottom strip of three cards should feel like a solid foundation — these are the pillars Meldar stands on.
- There should be a visual element or illustration that ties the three cards together — maybe a subtle connecting line, maybe an abstract visual in the background, maybe the cards themselves form a visual pattern.
- The whole thing should feel like one cohesive composition, not "headline + cards glued together."

## Technical constraints

- Must fit entirely in one viewport (no scrolling to see the three cards)
- Must work on desktop (1440px) and mobile (393px)
- The email capture field must be visible above the fold on all devices
- Navigation bar sits above this (72px tall, frosted glass)

## Current color palette (from Stitch's earlier work)

- Primary: #623153 (deep mauve)
- Gradient: #623153 → #FFB876 (mauve to golden)
- Surface: #faf9f6 (warm cream)

- Secondary text: #4f434a

## Current fonts

- Headlines: Bricolage Grotesque (bold, tight tracking)
- Body: Inter (light weight, clean)

## What to avoid

- AI-generated realistic images
- Stock photography
- Purple gradients on white (generic AI look)
- Robot or brain illustrations
- Anything that looks like a developer tool
- Visual complexity that scares non-technical people
- Generic SaaS hero layouts (centered text + phone mockup)

## What to explore

- Illustrations showing the three journeys (person entering AI, person discovering what to build, person with a stable app)
- Abstract visual metaphors for the three steps (door opening, lightbulb moment, solid foundation)
- The three bottom cards as an architectural element — maybe they look like building blocks, maybe they have a subtle 3D depth, maybe they connect visually
- Typography as the hero visual (like Leonardo.ai uses oversized text as texture)
- A single powerful illustration that captures all three pain points in one image
- Motion/animation that reveals the three cards as the page loads (staggered, purposeful, not gratuitous)
- The email capture as a design element, not an afterthought — maybe it has a distinctive shape or treatment that makes it feel special

## The feeling

When someone lands on this page, they should feel:
1. "Wait, that's exactly my problem" (recognition)
2. "These people actually understand" (trust)
3. "I want to try this right now" (action)

Not: "Oh, another AI tool." Not: "This looks complicated." Not: "I'll check this out later."

NOW. They should want to act NOW.
