# Social Media Strategy: Meldar Feature Review

**Role:** Social Media Strategist
**Features Under Review:** ChatGPT Export Analyzer (A), Subscription Autopsy (B), Actionable Screen Time (C), ADHD Mode (D)
**Target:** Gen Z (18-28), non-technical, daily ChatGPT users

---

## 1. TikTok Potential

### A. ChatGPT Export Analyzer -- HIGHEST TikTok potential

This is the TikTok goldmine. The ChatGPT export analyzer turns private AI habits into a public mirror, and mirrors go viral. The format practically writes itself:

- **"POV: I uploaded my ChatGPT history and..."** is a proven TikTok structure. The reveal format (build curiosity, then expose the embarrassing/surprising truth) is the #1 performing short-form content pattern.
- **Specific numbers make it real.** "I asked ChatGPT about meal planning 47 times and still order Wolt every night" -- that specificity is what gets comments like "literally me."
- **It exposes a universal Gen Z shame:** we all use ChatGPT, we all think we're being productive with it, and we're all secretly asking it the same things on repeat. Nobody talks about this yet. First mover advantage is massive.
- **Comment bait is built in.** People will tag friends, argue about their own patterns, request their own analysis. Every comment boosts reach.

Estimated virality window: 3-6 months before competitors copy the concept.

### B. Subscription Autopsy -- STRONG TikTok potential

Money content always performs, but "how much I waste" is a specific subgenre that already has proven traction (think: "I tracked every dollar I spent for a month"). The Subscription Autopsy fits cleanly into:

- **"I'm losing EUR __ per month on subscriptions I forgot about"** -- emotional, specific, actionable.
- **The graveyard visual** (cancelled vs. active, zombie subscriptions) is screenshot-friendly and works in static carousel format too.
- **Limitation:** subscription waste is already a known problem. The insight isn't as novel as the ChatGPT analyzer. It needs strong visual execution to stand out from Truebill/Rocket Money content.

### C. Actionable Screen Time -- MODERATE TikTok potential

Screen time content exists on TikTok but it has plateau'd. "My screen time is 8 hours" stopped being shocking. What Meldar can do differently is the **actionable** angle -- "Screen Time told me I use Instagram 4 hours. Meldar told me WHY and gave me a specific fix." That reframe could work, but it's a harder hook to land in 3 seconds.

### D. ADHD Mode -- IDENTITY content (different viral mechanics)

See Section 2 below. This is not a "reveal" format -- it's an identity/advocacy format, which has different (and potentially deeper) viral mechanics.

## 2. ADHD Mode as Identity Content

### The opportunity is enormous

ADHD content on TikTok is not a niche -- it is a cultural movement. The numbers:
- #ADHD has 35B+ views on TikTok
- ADHD creators consistently pull 1M+ views per video
- Late-diagnosis content, "things I thought were personality traits," and "apps that actually work for my ADHD brain" are perennial top performers

### "This app has an ADHD mode that doesn't shame you" is absolutely a viral moment

Here's why this specific framing works:

1. **It validates.** Most productivity apps implicitly shame neurodivergent users ("you failed your streak," "you didn't hit your goal"). Calling out that Meldar *explicitly* chose not to do this is a statement of values, not just a feature.

2. **It triggers the "FINALLY" response.** The most viral ADHD content creates a feeling of being seen. Comments like "WHY did it take until 2026 for someone to build this" drive massive engagement.

3. **Focus GIFs over streak counters** is a tangible, demonstrable difference. You can show this in a 15-second video. Concreteness matters -- vague "we're inclusive" messaging falls flat; specific design choices go viral.

4. **It crosses over.** ADHD content doesn't just reach people with diagnosed ADHD. It reaches the much larger group of people who suspect they might have it, people who relate to the symptoms, and allies. The addressable audience is 5-10x the diagnosed population.

### Risk to manage

The ADHD community is protective and skeptical of brands co-opting their identity. Meldar must:
- Have neurodivergent people involved in designing this mode (and say so publicly)
- Never position ADHD as a "problem to fix" -- frame it as a different operating system that deserves different tools
- Avoid "ADHD-washing" (slapping a label on basic features). The mode needs to be genuinely different in meaningful ways

If done authentically, this is Meldar's strongest long-term brand differentiator.

- **D:** The ADHD mode toggle screen, the "no shame" messaging, the focus GIF replacing a streak counter

## 4. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: "What Your Data Says About You"
The mirror pillar. Use ChatGPT Export Analyzer and Screen Time data to show people their own patterns. This is Meldar's "Spotify Wrapped" -- personalized content that people create for themselves and then share.
- Format: User-generated reveal videos, screenshot carousels, "I can't believe I..." reactions
- Cadence: Evergreen, spikes with feature launches

### Pillar 2: "The Money You're Losing"
The financial wake-up pillar. Subscription Autopsy + broader "Big Tech profits from you" narrative (the existing EUR 238 Google stat is perfect).
- Format: Split-screen comparisons, "what EUR X per month could buy instead," challenge videos
- Cadence: Monthly (tie to billing cycles, "new month new audit")

### Pillar 3: "Built Different (Literally)"
The ADHD/neurodivergent pillar. Position Meldar as the first productivity tool that respects different brains.
- Format: Side-by-side "other apps vs. Meldar," creator testimonials, design decision explainers
- Cadence: Ongoing, community-driven, respond to trending ADHD content

### Pillar 4: "Take Back Your Data"
The activism/empowerment pillar. Meldar's core brand story. Privacy-first, user-owned, anti-surveillance capitalism.
- Format: Educational ("did you know Google Takeout exists?"), empowerment ("your data, your rules"), "what we see vs. what we never see" trust content
- Cadence: Tie to news cycles (data breaches, privacy scandals, EU regulation updates)

### Pillar 5: "Meldar Fixed It"
The transformation pillar. Before/after stories from real users. "I was doom-scrolling 4 hours a day, Meldar showed me why and now..."
- Format: User testimonial UGC, founder story content, specific fix demonstrations
- Cadence: As user base grows, feature weekly success stories

## 7. Questions for the QA Agent

1. **ChatGPT Export privacy:** When a user uploads their ChatGPT export for analysis, where is the data processed? Is it fully client-side like the Google Takeout parser, or does it hit a server? This is the #1 question skeptical Gen Z users (and ADHD creators doing due diligence) will ask. If it touches a server, the viral narrative collapses.

2. **ADHD Mode depth:** What specifically changes in ADHD Mode beyond focus GIFs and shame-free framing? Does it change notification patterns, task chunking, information density, color schemes? The ADHD community will immediately test whether this is a genuine accommodation or a marketing label. We need substance behind the name.

3. **Subscription Autopsy data source:** How does Meldar detect subscriptions? Bank API integration, email receipt scanning, manual entry? The accuracy of the EUR total is critical -- if it misses subscriptions or double-counts, the viral screenshot moment becomes a credibility problem.

4. **Shareability infrastructure:** Are there built-in share cards/images for each feature's output? The ChatGPT Analyzer results and Subscription Autopsy totals need to be natively shareable as beautiful, branded images -- not just raw data screens. Does the current design include "share to TikTok/Instagram/Stories" functionality or exportable card formats?

5. **Content moderation for ChatGPT exports:** ChatGPT conversations can contain sensitive, personal, or NSFW content. If the analyzer surfaces patterns, could it accidentally expose something embarrassing in the summary that a user then shares publicly without realizing? Is there any filtering or sensitivity flagging in the analysis output?
